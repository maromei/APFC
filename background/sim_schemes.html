Simulation Schemes#

Just the amplitudes#

We use the gradient flow for the amplitudes (eq. (4)) and this fourier scheme. The linear and non linear part are chosen as follows:

(10)#\[\begin{split} \begin{gathered} \mathcal{L} = - |\boldsymbol{G}_m|^2 \left[ A \mathcal{G}^2 + B \right] \\ N = - |\boldsymbol{G}_m|^2 \left[ 3 D (\Phi - |\eta_m|^2) \eta_m + \frac{\partial f^s}{\partial \eta_m^*} \right] \end{gathered} \end{split}\]

Under the fourier transform in the linear part, \(B\) stays constant, and \(\mathcal{G}^2\) becomes:

(11)#\[\begin{split} \begin{align} \mathcal{G}_m &= \sqrt\beta \nabla^2 + 2 \mathbb{i} \boldsymbol{G}_m \nabla \\ \widehat{\mathcal{G}_m^2} &= \left[ \sqrt\beta\left(k_x^2 + k_y^2\right) + 2 G_m^{(x)} k_x + 2 G_m^{(y)} k_y \right]^2 \end{align} \end{split}\]

Where \(k_x\) and \(k_y\) are the frequency variables.

Amplitudes and average density#

For both the amplitudes and the densities this scheme is used.

For the amplitudes the linear and non-linear part can not be chosen in the same way as in eq. (10), since \(B\) contains \(n_0\). This is why \(B\) is split into the linear and non-linear parts:

(12)#\[\begin{split} \begin{gathered} \mathcal{L} = - |\boldsymbol{G}_m|^2 \left[ A \mathcal{G}^2 + \Delta B^0 \right] \\ N = - |\boldsymbol{G}_m|^2 \left[ (3 v n_0^2 - 2 t n_0) \eta_m + 3 D (\Phi - |\eta_m|^2) \eta_m + \frac{\partial f^s}{\partial \eta_m^*} \right] \end{gathered} \end{split}\]

For the average density (eq. (5)) only \((\Delta B^0 + B^x) n_0\) can be used as the linear part.

(13)#\[\begin{split} \begin{gathered} \mathcal{L} = \Delta B^0 + B^x \\ N = 3 v \Phi n_0 + 3 v P - \Phi t - t n_0^2 + v n_0^3 \end{gathered} \end{split}\]

The \(\nabla^2\) operator was excluded from the equation above. Since \(N\) needs to first be calculated and then be fourier-transformed, the nabla operator will just be applied after the transformation. Resulting in the scheme:

(14)#\[\begin{split} \begin{gathered} \widehat{\phi}_{t+1} = \frac{ \widehat{\phi}_t + \tau \widehat{\nabla^2} \widehat{N}_t } { 1 - \tau \widehat{\nabla^2} \widehat{\mathcal{L}} } \\ \text{with} \quad \widehat{\nabla^2} = -(k_x^2 + k_y^2) \end{gathered} \end{split}\]
